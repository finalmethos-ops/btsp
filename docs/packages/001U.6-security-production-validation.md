# 001U.6 — Security and Production Validation

## Outcome

001U.6 closes Epic 001U with least-privilege operational roles, enforced payment-approval separation of duties, cross-ledger integrity validation, and production operating boundaries.

## Roles

- `RECEIVING_OPERATOR`: receipt read/post within the assigned home store.
- `RECEIVING_MANAGER`: receipt operations plus variance and backorder resolution within the assigned home store.
- `AP_CLERK`: invoice read/ingestion and reconciliation visibility; cannot disposition or approve.
- `AP_APPROVER`: invoice/reconciliation visibility and reconciliation decisions; cannot ingest invoices.

System administration retains emergency platform access, but service-level separation of duties still prevents an actor from approving an invoice they ingested or a reconciliation case they created.

## Integrity validation

`scripts/validate_001u_integrity.py` performs a read-only audit of:

- accepted plus rejected receipt quantity equaling physical received quantity;
- non-negative and internally consistent backorder balances;
- invoice line and header arithmetic;
- approved cases having no open exceptions, a distinct approver, and an `approved_for_payment` invoice projection.

The command outputs aggregate counts and identifiers only on failure; it does not expose invoice documents or financial line details.

## Production boundary

Approval establishes BTSP authorization for downstream payment processing. BTSP does not transmit funds, post general-ledger entries, calculate tax liability, or replace an ERP/AP system. Production integration requires authenticated accounting export, delivery idempotency, reconciliation of acknowledgements, monitoring, and finance-owned access reviews.
